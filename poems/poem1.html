I woke alone at night in chains Of shining steel and weighted rings That hung from high in swollen veins, Or twist and caght like tattered wings. I knew the smell - the stench of death That rose and grew as vision blurred With every painful, broken breath, Until - at last - a shadow stirred. It stretched a pointed, stunted limb That held a sharpened shape. A sigh. A sound. A voice. A whisper, grim: 'You'll cross your heart and hope to die...'
